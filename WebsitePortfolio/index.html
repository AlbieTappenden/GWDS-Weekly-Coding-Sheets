Contact Me

Hello! My name is Albie Tappenden, I’m a student and I studied for 2 years at a Level 3 Diploma in ‘Animation for Games and Media’, in which I went on to achieving a Merit in both years. I am a 3D Generalist but specialise in hard surface modelling like vehicles and forms of weaponry. Using my skills, I am now studying ‘Digital Media’ at The University West of England in Bristol. See below to view some of my work from this year!

3D Models this year:

P5.js Creative Projects:

Repetition

For my first piece, I have taken inspiration from the Hungarian-french artist Victor Vasarely who specialized in creating art that tricked the eyes most commonly, but for this specific piece i recreated a simplified version of his style by using coloured squares and circles inside them. And how when the page is refreshed, each time the colours will be new.

Random

For my second, I was inspired by Sonia Delaunay, who was known for experimenting colours and shapes, with repetition to create visually appealing patterns. In my work I used code to randomize circles around the canvas as well as triangles with random rotation points. Again with my last piece my work is made so that when refreshed, it shows a new unique version whilst keeping all the colours.

Contrast

And for my final, I came up with this one myself, I realized for my other pieces i used randomization in colours but for this one i wanted to keep just 2 colours as well as keeping the objects the same when the page was refreshed. I tried to look at an artist called Josef Albers for the resizing of shapes but his stuff just didn't show off what I wanted to create.

Priniciples of 3D Projects:

3D Robot

For this project we were tasked with creating a stylised Robot in Autodesk Maya with a poly count of under 3000 polygons. Using my past skills it was a fun challenge as I hadnt made something with a maximum poly count before, but it was achievable and made me think more to be able to detail and still keep under the maximum, finishing the model with about 2,600 polys.

Custom Smart-Material

For the 3D model robot after fully creating it in Autodesk Maya and doing the UVs, I imported it into substance painter where i created all the textures for my robot, where i also created my own custom smart material using a base template of plastic scratches on a metal base, i changed the plastic to a more matte black to look like paint, and then swapped the metal to a darker shade of red with more metalness than roughness to give my robot a more of an evil look and like battle scars.

Graphic Compositions:

Cuboid optical

Starting off with my first composition I wanted to create a 3D Cube and did so with simple lines, After creating it I then added the 3 primary colours and gave each line a bit of a bend to confuse the brain on how to see if the cube is being displayed inwards or outwards.

3D Star

In my second composition I took multiple triangles and alligned and deformed each one so that the inside took the shape of a star, then gave from bottom to top a dark shade to a lighter grade to give a sense of lighting.

Cube Illusion

For my third Composition I took lots of squares and overlapped each one of them then gave some of them an outline effect, to create a sense of 3D in a fully 2D image.

Web Design-

Starting off this project and overall module the worksheets were a big challenge, as learning multiple coding languages at once for other projects confused me at points of not knowing why i was having errors then realising i had just got the formatting wtrong, despite that having the worksheets was a massive increase to my skills and confidence when creating my own website, using the bootstrap template so i could use it for the responsivness of my website when resized on desktop and different sized monitors, using the bootstrap I also used the columns that were already in the template along with the titles. I researched other ways of adding details like a gradient fade for the top navbar but then didnt like it after viewing it on other sizes and so removed it. I then learnt when adding padding what px and py meant, because before we were only taught what py-3 was, and I didnt understand what it was but then when i researched how to add padding onto the x axis of text i learnt that py was the y value and px is the x value, and so i added py-3 and px-3 along with a border to most of my text descriptions for easier readibility.

When it came to creating the about me section I stumped at first on how to make sure the box would stick to the bottom of the page but after a bit of researching I easily found out how to do it along with a way to make it so when the user clicks on the names of my contacting ways, they would be redirected to the site, or, in my opinion the coolest one i found out, was when you click on my email option, you are sent to your emails and it automatically puts my email into the user you send an email to. Adding onto when I made the contact page I realised that on my navbar i had the contact me button but when clicking it it didn't actually go anywhere so I talked to my coursemate who knew how to do it and he taught me how to get it to work, in which i used that new skill to add it again under my contact page with a button that would scroll for the user back to the top of the page.
